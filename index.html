Hi! I'm Anna Rahn, a B.S./M.S. student at the University of Colorado Boulder. I have two research areas: using computational neuroscience to study perceptions of children's interactions with technology in the Brain AI and Child (BAIC) Lab (Department of Computer Science) and developing reinforcement learning models of biological systems, such as fireflies and dog-sheep herding. (BioFrontiers Institute). I also currently serve on the board of the Rocky Mountain AI Interest Group (RMAIIG), CU's chapter of the Society of Women Engineers, and the International Festival Committee.

How did I get here? In 6th grade, I wrote my first program using Hopscotch. I clicked "Run" and a simple sprite moved left, left, right. The instant, visual results helped me understand how computers execute instructions while the gameified environment encouraged me to learn increasingly complex topics. Additionally, the block-based language greatly helped me in understanding code structure. By the end of 8th grade, I had written choose-your-own-adventure stories, drawing programs, and copycat games such as Geometry Dash. In high school, I then became involved with FIRST Robotics, CyberPatriot, and the National Technical Honor Society. The success of these activites helped me develop the confidence to continue pursuing the subject.

I am also particularly interested in landscape photography - see my work on @packingpostcards on Instagram and here. Message me with inquiries!
